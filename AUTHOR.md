# About the Author

## Douglas P. Galullo

Douglas P. Galullo is the founder of Dog House Ventures LLC and the author of the responsibility infrastructure thesis presented in this repository.

His analytical perspective was shaped through work inside multiple operational environments, including:

- machinery operation and repair,
- manufacturing,
- law enforcement,
- postal operations,
- frontline supervision,
- business ownership,
- software development,
- and applied artificial intelligence workflows.

These environments required rapid decisions under incomplete information, close attention to authority and procedure, exception handling, evidence preservation, process continuity, and personal accountability for real-world outcomes.

Across these different roles, the recurring pattern was not simply learning a new job. It was learning how the surrounding process actually functioned:

- where work entered,
- how it moved,
- which constraints were fixed,
- where discretion existed,
- where friction accumulated,
- what happened after the immediate decision,
- and who ultimately carried responsibility.

In legally or institutionally defined systems, such as law enforcement and postal operations, the objective was not to casually change the governing process. It was to understand the process, respect its authority and limits, and execute effectively within its lawful boundaries.

In adaptable operational systems, the same observation could be used to identify friction, test improvements, and refine procedures.

That process-oriented perspective became the lens through which AI-assisted work was studied and ultimately led to the responsibility infrastructure hypothesis.
