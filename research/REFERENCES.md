# Research and Sourcing Agenda

The initial article is an original thesis essay and does not yet contain a formal academic bibliography.

Before submission to a fact-checking publication or academic venue, the following areas should receive independent sourcing and review.

## AI adoption and investment

Evidence concerning:

- expansion of AI use in consequential work,
- organizational adoption,
- investment trends,
- and movement from assistance toward greater operational autonomy.

## Activity logs and control evidence

Research distinguishing:

- event or activity logging,
- audit evidence,
- authorization records,
- control evidence,
- and reconstructable decision records.

## Human oversight

Research on:

- automation bias,
- rubber-stamp approval,
- meaningful human review,
- human factors in automated decision systems,
- and the limits of nominal human-in-the-loop controls.

## Governance and internal controls

Relevant frameworks concerning:

- separation of duties,
- documented authority,
- decision rights,
- approval controls,
- escalation,
- exception handling,
- and institutional recordkeeping.

## Procedure and change control

Standards and research concerning:

- controlled SOP revision,
- document versioning,
- effective dates,
- supersession,
- approval history,
- and preserving the reason for procedural change.

## Liability and professional responsibility

Research addressing whether existing:

- liability law,
- professional standards,
- contracts,
- insurance,
- and regulatory obligations

allocate blame after a failure but still leave gaps in reconstructing how AI-assisted work was authorized, evidenced, reviewed, and accepted while it was occurring.

## Research principle

No source should be added merely because it appears to support the thesis. Relevant counterarguments, conflicting evidence, and limitations should be preserved alongside supporting material.
