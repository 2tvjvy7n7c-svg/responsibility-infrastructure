# Future Papers

This repository begins with one focused thesis. Related ideas will be developed separately so they do not overload or dilute the first article.

## Paper 2 - Process Before Theory

### Working question

How can complex systems be examined from inside the work rather than only through outputs, diagrams, or formal descriptions?

### Proposed scope

- studying how a process actually flows,
- distinguishing fixed constraints from adaptable procedures,
- rapid judgment under incomplete information,
- tracing second-, third-, and later-order consequences,
- identifying friction, handoff failure, and procedural drift,
- controlled intervention,
- repeated observation,
- and converting validated learning into governed SOPs.

This paper will examine the analytical method that produced the responsibility infrastructure thesis.

It will not be framed primarily as an AI paper.

## Paper 3 - Responsibility Infrastructure

### Working question

What organizational and technical structure is required to connect mandate, authority, evidence, review, decisions, procedural change, and final acceptance in consequential AI-assisted work?

### Proposed scope

- mandate and warrant,
- evidence-conditioned authority,
- bounded roles,
- meaningful challenge,
- responsibility records,
- supersession and recall,
- exception handling,
- institutional memory,
- formal acceptance,
- and proportional governance.

This paper will move from the high-level thesis toward a framework others can evaluate and challenge.

## Separation principle

Paper 1 defines the problem.

Paper 2 explains the process-analysis method that revealed it.

Paper 3 develops the proposed responsibility infrastructure framework.
