## Why this milestone is recorded

Proof Ready is Dog House Ventures’ commercial product direction for turning scattered records, claims, documents, photos, communications, and timelines into **reviewer-ready proof packages** — answering what is claimed, what evidence supports it, what is missing, what conflicts, how strong the proof is, and what package a reviewer can receive.

On 2026-07-17, the company sealed an **implementation readiness baseline** while keeping a hard gate: **production implementation is not authorized** until market and buyer evidence pass explicit thresholds. Publishing a **HOLD** is more credible than silent vaporware progress.

---

## Product thesis and boundary (public)

- Proof Ready organizes evidence and proof readiness; it is **not** legal, tax, insurance, investment, or compliance advice.  
- It does not determine coverage, liability, damages, settlement value, legal admissibility, or professional sufficiency.  
- **Primary validation lane:** insurance-claim proof packages.  
- Other lanes (e.g. property management) may be watched or held without equal investment until evidence justifies them.

### GO evidence thresholds (counts only; interview content internal)

The gate requires, among other items, evidence at roughly these thresholds before a GO decision:

- Multiple graded sources proving a repeated documentation problem.  
- Sources proving spend, budget path, or paid workaround.  
- Direct buyer/problem interviews (plan targets substantially more than the minimum).  
- Interviewee signals on willingness to pay or referral, and price/budget triggers.  
- Competitor evidence of a narrow reviewer-ready proof-package gap.  
- Written privacy / advice-boundary review.  
- Written GO / CONDITIONAL GO / NO-GO by the product owner.

**Permitted before GO:** interviews, workflow mapping, architecture options, threat modeling, low-fidelity UX, disposable synthetic-data spikes, acceptance criteria and test planning.  
**Prohibited before GO:** production feature development; intake of real claim documents / personal / privileged material; public promises about claim outcomes or insurer acceptance; automated legal/insurance advice.
